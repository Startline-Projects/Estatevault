## SECTION 2 — AUTHORIZATION FOR USE AND DISCLOSURE

### 2.1 — Description of Protected Health Information.

I authorize the use and disclosure of all of my protected health information, including but not limited to: medical records and clinical notes from all healthcare providers who have provided care to me; laboratory test results, imaging studies, and diagnostic reports; prescription and medication records, including pharmacy records; mental health and substance abuse treatment records (subject to the additional protections of federal law where applicable, including 42 C.F.R. Part 2 for substance abuse records); hospital admission, discharge, and treatment records; records relating to communicable diseases, including HIV/AIDS; genetic testing results and family medical history; insurance billing and payment records; records of communications between healthcare providers and other authorized parties about my care; and any other records relating to my physical or mental health, healthcare, or payment for healthcare services. This authorization covers all such information regardless of the date the information was created or the date the medical service was provided.

### 2.2 — Covered Entities Authorized to Disclose.

I authorize the following classes of persons and entities to disclose my protected health information: all healthcare providers, including physicians, dentists, optometrists, mental health professionals, chiropractors, podiatrists, nurses, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and other licensed healthcare professionals who have provided care to me; all hospitals, clinics, surgery centers, urgent care facilities, nursing homes, rehabilitation facilities, hospices, and other healthcare institutions where I have received care; all pharmacies that have filled prescriptions for me; all laboratories, imaging centers, and diagnostic facilities that have performed tests on me; all health insurance companies, health maintenance organizations, and other insurers that have covered my healthcare; all government healthcare programs that have provided benefits to me, including Medicare, Medicaid, the Veterans Health Administration, and Tricare; and any other person or entity that holds my protected health information in connection with any aspect of my healthcare or payment for healthcare.

### 2.3 — Excluded Records.

This authorization does NOT cover psychotherapy notes as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.501. Under 45 C.F.R. § 164.508(b)(3), psychotherapy notes require a separate, single-purpose authorization that cannot be combined with other authorizations. If I wish to authorize disclosure of my psychotherapy notes, I will execute a separate authorization for that purpose.

## SECTION 5 — EXPIRATION

This authorization expires on {{hipaa_expiration_date}}, which is seven (7) years from the date I sign this authorization, unless I revoke it earlier in writing or unless I execute a new authorization that supersedes this one.

If I wish to extend the authorization, I may execute a new authorization at any time. The new authorization replaces this one as to any new disclosures after the date of the new authorization.

## SECTION 6 — REVOCATION RIGHTS

### 6.1 — Right to Revoke.

I understand that I have the right to revoke this authorization at any time, except to the extent that a covered entity has already taken action in reliance on this authorization before receiving my written revocation. To revoke this authorization, I must submit a written revocation to the relevant covered entity in one of the following manners: hand delivery to the covered entity's Privacy Officer; certified mail (return receipt requested) to the covered entity's Privacy Officer at the address provided on the covered entity's HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices; or any other method permitted by the covered entity's policies, as set forth in the covered entity's HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices.

A written revocation is effective when received by the covered entity. Revocation is not retroactive: a covered entity that has already disclosed PHI in reliance on this authorization before receiving the written revocation is not liable for that disclosure.

### 6.2 — Limitation on Revocation.

Revocation does not affect disclosures already made in reliance on this authorization before the covered entity received the written revocation, disclosures required by law (court order, subpoena, or other legal process), or disclosures necessary to allow the covered entity to comply with the Privacy Rule itself.

## SECTION 7 — REQUIRED STATEMENTS UNDER 45 C.F.R. § 164.508

### 7.1 — Treatment, Payment, Enrollment, and Eligibility.

My signing of this authorization is voluntary. No healthcare provider, insurer, or other covered entity may condition my treatment, my payment, my enrollment in a health plan, or my eligibility for benefits on my signing this authorization, except in the limited circumstances permitted by 45 C.F.R. § 164.508(b)(4) (relating to research-related treatment, eligibility for benefits underwritten by the insurer who is requesting the authorization, healthcare consisting solely of creation of PHI for disclosure to a third party, and certain other limited situations).

### 7.2 — Right to Receive a Copy.

I have the right to receive a copy of this signed authorization. By signing this authorization, I acknowledge that I have been offered a copy.

### 7.3 — Redisclosure of Information.

I understand that protected health information disclosed pursuant to this authorization may be subject to redisclosure by the recipient and may no longer be protected by federal privacy regulations. Once protected health information is disclosed to a person who is not a covered entity under HIPAA, the recipient is not bound by HIPAA's privacy and security rules with respect to the disclosed information.
